GIS Technician, Cornwallis Headwaters Society

Description:

Following my technical geomatics training, I spent 2011-2013 working for a non-profit environmental group called the Cornwallis Headwaters Society (CHS), which researched the health of the Cornwallis River headwaters in the Annapolis Valley, Nova Scotia.

During my time with CHS, I was responsible for the collection and management of spatial data for the research group, developing maps relating to the watershed's boundaries and agricultural landscape, web development, and also surveyed culverts for a fish passage study.

Work Requirements:
  • Watershed research
  • ArcGIS
  • Spatial Analyst
  • Watershed and agriculture field boundary map creation
  • Field data collection
  • Data management using file geodatabases and MS Access
  • Web development
  • Culvert assessment
