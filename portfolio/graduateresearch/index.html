Cybercartographic Graduate Research

Description:

The focus of my graduate research at Carleton University, was a multi-method geomatics approach in the spatial analysis of Roman building patterns in Britain. It utilized the strengths of different geomatics tools, to model the decision process that Romans took when selecting a suitable site for a Roman fortification.

The research used the flexibility of the cybercartographic framework Nunaliit for data collection of known archaeological site locations, data visualization (which incorporated the d3js library), and was also used for sharing research. Modelling of Roman building practices was performed using GIS with fuzzy set theory, which refined the search area for potential Roman archaeological fortification sites in Britain. With the valid search areas determined, selected locations were assessed for Roman occupation potential and a final area was selected for imagery analysis. The final image analysis utilized WorldView 2 multispectral satellite imagery with the goal of observing surface patterns that could suggest the presence of sub-surface Roman building features.

The research atlas can currently be viewed here.

Project Requirements:
  • Cybercartographic and historical research
  • Project management and independent research
  • JavaScript development using the Nunaliit SDK
  • Data visualization with D3.js
  • Data management using CouchDB, shape files, and raster layers
  • Spatial analysis in ArcGIS using Fuzzy Set Theory for model development
  • WorldView 2 multispectral imagery analysis
